Day 1 · June 20

The First Morning in Nice

📍 Vieux Nice · 26°C

We got to Nice around noon. The old town hits you immediately. These narrow streets with faded ochre buildings, shutters half open, laundry hanging between balconies. Cours Saleya was packed with flower vendors and the whole street smelled like lavender and fresh bread. I lost Courtney for twenty minutes. Found her holding a bag of soap she definitely didn't need. We ended up at this bistro halfway up the Colline du Château where the waiter kept bringing us things we didn't order and waving off every attempt to pay for them.

Day 2 · June 21

The Croisette and Every Yacht

📍 La Croisette, Cannes · 28°C

The train from Nice hugs the coast the entire way. Thirty minutes of cliffs dropping straight into turquoise water, little towns flashing past with orange roofs and church steeples. You step off in Cannes and it immediately feels different. Wider streets, bigger sunglasses, more attitude.

forty photos of this exact view

We walked the Croisette end to end. Courtney counted every yacht in the harbor. I told her that's not a personality trait. She said neither is whatever I was doing on my phone. Fair enough.

Day 3 · June 22

Èze, Eventually

📍 Èze · 24°C

Google Maps sent us through someone's actual backyard. Like, there were chickens. Courtney was dying. We rerouted twice, took a bus that felt like it was held together by optimism, and climbed the last stretch on foot. But then you come around this corner and the whole Mediterranean just opens up below you. Four hundred meters straight down to the water.

the reason we climbed

The village itself is all stone walls and winding paths and cactus gardens clinging to the cliff. We found a spot at the top with two chairs and no one around and just sat there. You could see all the way to where the sky meets the water. On a clear day they say you can see Corsica.

Two perspectives, one moment
Spencer

She went quiet at the top. Not bad quiet. The kind where someone's actually seeing something. Took a photo of her instead of the view.

Courtney

I caught him taking a photo of me staring at the view. He tried to play it off. He did not play it off.
